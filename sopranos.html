The Sopranos¶

Description¶

An American crime drama television series created by David Chase. The series follows Tony Soprano (James Gandolfini), a New Jersey Mafia boss who has panic attacks. He reluctantly begins seeing psychiatrist Dr. Jennifer Melfi (Lorraine Bracco), who encourages him to open up about his difficulties balancing his family life with his criminal life. Other important characters include Tony's family, Mafia colleagues, and rivals, most notably his wife Carmela (Edie Falco), his nephew and protégé Christopher Moltisanti (Michael Imperioli), and his uncle Corrado "Junior" Soprano (Dominic Chianese).

Viewership¶

The Sopranos viewership generally rose from modest early Season 1 numbers to its highest levels around Seasons 3 and 4. Season 4 contains the peak episode, reaching over 13 million viewers. After that, viewership gradually declined through Seasons 5 and 6A, mostly staying below earlier highs. Season 6B was lower overall, but the finale produced a major spike, showing renewed audience interest at the end of the series.

Changes in viewership¶

The Sopranos viewership was highly variable, with frequent increases and decreases between consecutive episodes. The largest positive change occurred around the transition into S04E01, where viewership increased by almost 4 million. Another major increase happened at the end of the series, from S06BE08 to S06BE09, where viewership rose by nearly 3.9 million.

The largest drops also occurred after major spikes. For example, after S04E01, viewership fell by a little over 2 million in the next episode. Earlier in the series, there were also sharp declines, including a drop of more than 2 million for S02E02 and another drop of about 3 million for S03E03. Later seasons show smaller episode-to-episode movements overall, especially through much of Season 6A, where changes mostly stayed close to zero.
